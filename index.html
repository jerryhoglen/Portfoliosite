Who I am


"Solution oriented versus excuse driven."


Coder with a background in retail and business management. I have always been passionate about solving problems. I have spent 10 years working in retail and business management and have been successful by always challenging myself to create solutions to the problems my teams face daily. I grew up around technology my whole life but started coding as a hobby about a year ago. It quickly became something I knew I wanted to learn more about so I joined the CareerFoundry web development bootcamp in August 2016. I am very excited to continue building and learning as much as possible.

I am currently working on my freelance web business but interested in exploring any opportunities that align with my culture and values. I am committed to learning new technologies and mastering the skills that I have learned. I have worked on and led many teams and consider myself to be a well rounded team player that possesses many leadership qualities needed to ensure products delivered exceed expectations.

Skills

My focus is on front-end web development. I am passionate about design and development and I think it's important for coders to be interested and involved in the design process so they understand and can deliver a product that meets the customer and designers needs. The course I took through CareerFoundry was a full-stack web development course that used Ruby on Rails for the back end. I did enjoy learning about Ruby on Rails and I believe it's important as a developer to have an understanding of back end web development. I would consider myself to be experienced in HTML and CSS, and intermediate in Javascript and Ruby on Rails. I am also experienced in using the bootstrap framework but I can build fully responsive websites without the need for frameworks. When it comes to content management systems I am experienced in Wordpress and Squarespace.


Previous work

Responsive Portfolio Website

This is an interactive responsive website that I built during my full stack web development certification at Careerfoundry. This site was built using the bootstrap framework and uses HTML5, Javascript and Jquery. It also has the google maps api integrated into the contact section.

Ruby on Rails e-commerce website

This is an e-commerce retail bike shop built with Ruby on Rails. Key features of this site are create user and admin sign ups with the ability to post, edit, delete comments and ratings. Users can log in and log out and receive email upon sign up. The reviews and ratings use AJAX to show new comments and reviews were added with out full page reload. The site has fully functioning ability to accept payment through stripe.
